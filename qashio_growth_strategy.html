OverviewExecutive Summary

Who Qashio is today — and why the next 18 months are critical.

What Qashio Is

Qashio is a Dubai-born, globally licensed B2B spend management platform founded in 2021 by CEO Armin Moradi and co-founder Jonathan Lau. It provides corporate prepaid cards, expense management, and — crucially — the largest B2B loyalty ecosystem in MENA, with exclusive airline and hotel partnerships that no competitor has replicated.


With offices in Dubai, Abu Dhabi, Riyadh, Amman, and Dublin (opened Dec 2025), and a European e-money license enabling EU passporting to 27 countries, Qashio is uniquely positioned as a cross-continental fintech bridge between MENA and Europe.

The Business Today

  • Core product: Customised prepaid Visa corporate cards + spend controls
  • Loyalty engine: Earn points → redeem for Emirates Skywards miles, KLM Flying Blue, British Airways Avios, Jumeirah, Accor, IHG
  • Corporate travel: Second revenue line, emerging product
  • European arm: E-money institution license (Ireland → EU passporting), targeting UK and EEA
  • Recent raise: $19.8M led by Rocketship.vc (May 2025); additional $12.5M debt from Al-Enma Bank (Sep 2025)
  • Target markets: UAE ✓ → Saudi Arabia (launching) → Jordan ✓ → Europe ✓
  • Verticals: Legal, consulting, government, travel, hospitality, e-commerce

Bottom Line: Qashio is profitable, growing 800% YoY for three straight years, and sitting on a moat no competitor has — an airline-grade B2B loyalty engine. The question is not whether Qashio will grow. The question is how fast and in which direction to make it fundable at a Series B valuation.

Working Capital for Corporate Travel — B2B BNPL

Revenue Expansion Lending Product 12–18 Months

Corporate travel is expensive and lumpy. A company's travel budget runs out before quarter-end, then spikes at conference season. Qashio's existing debt facility (Al-Enma Bank) is a foundation for building embedded working capital into the card — extend credit at the moment of booking, repaid from the company's expense settlement cycle.

  • Offer "Qashio Credit" — a revolving credit line sized to historical corporate card spend
  • Charge 1–2% on outstanding balance (still cheaper than bank overdraft)
  • Particularly valuable for SMEs with uneven cash flow
  • Travel BNPL: book flights now, pay in 30/60 days — Qashio earns interchange + interest
  • The data advantage: Qashio knows each company's spend patterns better than any bank — making credit underwriting safer

The Pivotal Moment
In 2022, Brex hit a wall: $312M ARR growing only 30% YoY while burning $22M/month. They made a brutal decision — fire their SMB customer base (startups) and go all-in on enterprise.
