What Stitch Is

Stitch is an operating system for modern financial institutions — a cloud-native, API-first platform that replaces fragmented, decades-old core banking infrastructure. The tagline: "The operating system every bank wishes it had built 20 years ago."

Founded in Riyadh, Saudi Arabia in 2022 by Mohamed Oueida, Stitch provides a single modular stack that lets banks, fintechs, and non-financial companies launch financial products 80% faster than legacy systems — going live in under 90 days.

Product Modules

  • Lending — Origination, underwriting, disbursement, servicing, collections; Islamic murabaha-compatible
  • Cards — Prepaid, debit, credit card programs (physical + virtual)
  • Wallets — P2P transfers, bill payments, multi-currency
  • Deposits — Savings, term deposits, demand deposit accounts
  • Payments — Unified switch across cards, bank transfers, wallets
  • Ledgers — Double-entry, multi-currency, real-time, programmable
  • BNPL Software — Infrastructure for institutions to launch their own BNPL

The Core Thesis

Financial institutions run on fragmented legacy infrastructure, and AI on top of broken infrastructure is a dead end — which is why Stitch was built to fix that.

— Mohamed Oueida, Founder & CEO

The Gulf is a rare greenfield for core replacements. What Stitch is building — a modern, unified system of record — is what makes everything else possible.

Business Model

SaaS + API pricing — no per-transaction fees, predictable expansion-friendly structures. Customers can adopt modules independently — no big-bang core replacement required.

Geopolitical Context & Risk Map

The region is navigating significant geopolitical turbulence. Understanding how it affects Stitch's funding narrative and customer pipeline is critical for the meeting.

🔴
Gaza Conflict & Regional Instability High Impact
MENA startup funding fell 37% YoY in Q1 2026 directly correlated with heightened geopolitical risk. The West Bank economy contracted 28% in 2024. However, GCC specifically has proven more resilient — Saudi Arabia and UAE are insulated by oil revenue, government spending, and Vision 2030 mandates. Capital is becoming more selective but continues to flow into infrastructure and B2B tools that are recession-resistant. The conflict is actually accelerating some digital finance adoption as businesses hedge physical risks by digitizing financial operations.
🟡
Red Sea Trade Disruptions Medium Impact
Elevated freight costs and shipping route risks from Houthi attacks. This increases the urgency of digital trade finance and cross-border payment infrastructure — directly relevant to Stitch's remittances and payments infrastructure customers (LuLu Exchange). Supply chain disruptions are pushing companies to digitize financial supply chains faster, which is net positive for infrastructure vendors.
🟡
Iran-Israel Tensions Medium Impact
Multinationals are reassessing GCC exposure and some Western VCs are taking a more cautious approach to MENA. However, this is creating a narrative that local GCC-native founders and infrastructure are actually a better bet — less geopolitical risk transfer, more aligned with the local regulatory environment. a16z's decision to make their first GCC deal now (amid tensions) signals conviction, not caution. Stitch's local Saudi registration and government-backed SVC investor are competitive advantages in this environment.
🟡
Iran Sanctions & Cross-Border Payment Complexity Medium Impact
Iran sanctions create compliance complexity for any financial infrastructure touching SWIFT or dollar-denominated flows. Stitch's infrastructure will need robust sanctions screening. However, the region's move toward local payment systems (mada, UAE NAPS, Arab Monetary Fund's Buna) is creating a new rail independent of Western sanctions pressure. This is actually a market tailwind for region-native infrastructure.
🟢
Saudi Vision 2030 — Policy-Driven Boom Strategic Opportunity
Vision 2030 creates government-mandated demand for fintech infrastructure that transcends market cycles. SAMA is issuing new licenses (digital banks, BNPL, open banking, consumer finance) at an unprecedented pace. Each new licensee needs core banking infrastructure. The government itself (through SVC, PIF/Sanabil) is providing anchor capital that de-risks private investment. Stitch is uniquely positioned as a Saudi-HQ company with government investor alignment.
🟢
AI Transformation Pressure Strategic Opportunity
55% of Gulf financial institutions miss growth opportunities due to legacy technology. With AI adoption now a board-level mandate across GCC banks, the infrastructure modernization narrative has shifted from "nice to have" to existential urgency. This is compressing the sales cycle and increasing willingness to pay — and Stitch's positioning as "AI-ready infrastructure" directly addresses the primary obstacle.
🟢
GCC Remittances — Massive Underdigitized Market Strategic Opportunity
GCC workers sent $131.5B in remittances in 2023 — the largest remittance source globally. Saudi expats alone sent $38B+ in 2024. Only 15% of outward remittances are digital vs. 52% global average. Stitch's customer LuLu Exchange is already tapping this. The gap between 15% and 52% represents ~$90B in potential digital remittance volume that needs modern infrastructure.

Bottom Line for the Meeting

Geopolitical turbulence is creating a flight to quality within the GCC. Investors want recession-resistant, infrastructure-layer, recurring-revenue businesses with government alignment and local regulatory expertise. Stitch ticks every single box. The timing of the a16z deal (immediately after Q1 2026 slowdown) is a deliberate signal. Present ideas that reinforce this narrative — not ideas that expand risk exposure.

Stitch should create a "New Bank Starter Kit" — a pre-configured, pre-certified, SAMA-compliant core banking bundle that reduces time-to-license from 12+ months to 60 days.
