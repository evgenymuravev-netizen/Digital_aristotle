N°01 Thesis

Traditional trade runs on cash and trust — and the credit that reaches it arrives late, bolted onto someone else's sale, chasing repayment after the fact. So it loses. Put the credit on a rail it can't fall off, collect where the money already moves, and the oldest business in the market becomes the most legible one. That is the whole idea.

Own the flow · out-underwrite the field · sell the machine

N°02 The binding constraint

Collections is theboss battle.

Demand isn't the problem. Data isn't the problem. The thing that kills lenders in this segment is repayment capture — and almost everyone solves it last.

Solve it structurally and approval aggression, pricing and funding cost all get easier downstream. Solve it last and you spend your life chasing cash.

  1. 01

    Cash-dominant, thin-file

    Millions of shops buy daily in cash. Invisible to a bank — fully legible to the flow.

  2. 02

    Credit that chases

    Bolt it onto a sale you don't own and you collect after the fact. The classic loss driver.

  3. 03

    Losses lag growth

    Defaults trail originations by 6–12 months. Outrun the seasoning and the book hides its rot.

  4. →

    The structural answer

    Don't chase. Own the rail repayment travels on and capture it at the source of the merchant's cash.

¹ The pattern holds on four continents — flow-repaid embedded credit is a proven, profitable shape. The failures share one trait: scale that outran control.
