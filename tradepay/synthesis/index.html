The problem

Collections is the boss battle.

Traditional trade runs on trust and cash. When credit arrives, it's bolted onto someone else's transaction — and spends its life chasing repayment after the fact. That detached collection is the single biggest reason books in this segment die.

01

Cash-dominant, thin-file

Millions of shops buy daily from distributors in cash. No formal statements, no bureau depth — invisible to a bank, perfectly legible to the flow.

02

Credit that chases

Bolt credit onto a transaction you don't own and you collect after the fact — the classic loss driver in informal trade.

03

Speed kills the careless

Losses lag originations by 6–12 months. Growing faster than the book seasons is how this category's failures all died.

→

The answer is structural

Don't chase repayment. Own the rail it travels on. Capture it at the source of the merchant's cash, and lending to the thin-file works.
