01 / The Problem

In cash-dominant trade, collections is the boss battle.

Saudi traditional trade runs on cash, trust and the rep's notebook. Merchants are thin-file to banks, distributors carry informal credit they can't price, and every lender who bolted credit onto someone else's transaction has had to chase repayment after the fact — the loss driver that kills books in this segment.

FOR THE MERCHANT

Invisible to banks, capped by cash

A shop turning SAR 50–500K a month has no formal credit history. Stock-ups for Ramadan are funded from the till or not at all. The cost isn't interest — it's the empty shelf.

FOR THE DISTRIBUTOR

Credit desk by intuition

Distributors already extend informal terms — underwritten by a rep's memory, collected on the route, written off quietly. Building an in-house financing arm takes 18–24 months and still doesn't solve collections.

FOR THE LENDER

Detached credit dies chasing cash

Capiter blitz-scaled to 50,000 merchants and collapsed in 2022. Losses lag originations by 6–12 months; credit that doesn't own the repayment flow learns this the expensive way.

The structural answer: don't chase the cash — own the rail it moves on. When repayment is a ledger event on a settlement rail you operate, collections stops being a department and becomes a property of the system.
02 / The Product

One company, three chapters.

Own the flow. Out-underwrite everyone. Then sell the machine. Each chapter manufactures the asset the next one needs — which is why the order is the strategy.

CHAPTER 1 · 2026 — Y1

The Rail

A settlement rail between anchor distributors and their merchants. Orders are financed on the rail; repayment is captured at the source via sarie standing transfers, wallet auto-debit or rep cash-in — netted across suppliers into one due date. Murabaha-native, no late fees, rebates for early settlement.

Who pays: the distributor is settled T+1 and carries zero credit risk; Tradepay earns the financing margin on flow it can actually collect.

SURFACES — rep tablet SDK · distributor console · WhatsApp · assisted onboarding

04 / 2026 → 2031

The product evolves in the only order that compounds.

This is not a feature roadmap — it's a dependency chain. Each chapter manufactures the asset the next one needs, and nothing ships ahead of its evidence. Scrub the years:

OWN THE FLOW→ COLLECTIONS PROOF→ PROPRIETARY DATA→ ENGINE EDGE→ FUNDED TRACK RECORD→ RENTABLE MACHINE

2026 — Prove the rail in one city

What ships: settlement rail MVP with two anchor distributors; rep-assisted merchant onboarding (Nafath + PDPL + Murabaha one-pager); financed orders with partial approvals; repayment wallet (sarie / auto-debit / rep cash-in); distributor console with AR & collections workflow; WhatsApp nudges.

Why this first: collections is the binding constraint of the whole category — not demand, not data, not capital. Square lent $22B+ at <3% loss only because repayment rides its own rail; Capiter died chasing cash it didn't control. Until repayment capture is proven, every later product is a liability multiplier. The rail also quietly starts three clocks: the data moat, the loan tape, and the distributor relationship the Arm will later monetize.

Deliberately NOT built yet: a merchant self-serve app (acquisition stays channel-led until trust exists), a second city (the Capiter rule), and any white-label offer (nobody rents an unproven machine).

2027–28 — Compound the engine, widen the rail

What ships: merchant app (limits, one-tap repay, portable record); multi-distributor netting — one limit, five suppliers, one due date; engine analytics for partners with the audited “+15pp at your loss” test; marketplace checkout widget; first bank warehouse facility; engine-as-a-service pilot on an external book.

Why now and not before: the rail's Year-1 exhaust — settlement truth, repayment behavior, ZATCA flows — is the feature set no off-rail lender can copy. The cheapest growth is not more cities; it's approving more of the same flow at the same loss. Approval-at-constant-loss becomes a provable commercial weapon that wins every distributor and marketplace conversation, and a clean, seasoned tape makes banks fund the book (the Lendo playbook), so capital stops being the ceiling.

Why the app arrives second: trust precedes surface. Merchants meet Tradepay inside a relationship they already trust (the rep); the app is the retention and transparency layer once they're transacting — not an acquisition bet against the channel.

2029–31 — Open the machine

What ships: the Infrastructure Arm — a white-label program console where a distributor launches its branded financing arm on Tradepay's rail, engine, Shariah governance and capital access in six weeks; Murabaha securitization of seasoned receivables; UAE entry partner-led, then Egypt; aggregated, PDPL-clean trade intelligence for FMCG brands.

Why last — and why inevitable: distributor in-house arms get built either way; the only question is on whose machine. By 2029 Tradepay has what no in-house build can match: two years of audited collections, a measured approval edge, and funded vintages. Tenants get decisions, rails and governance — never features, weights or pooled cohort data — so every tenant widens the engine's lead instead of arming a rival. Infrastructure revenue is additive: it monetizes flow Tradepay would never originate directly.

Expansion discipline: one market's full loop proven before the next opens. The country-launch kit makes UAE a 1–2 quarter deployment; the gates make it safe. Expansion is a consequence of proof, never a substitute for it.

08 / Trust & Compliance

Genuine structure is easy to be transparent about.

SHARIAH

Murabaha-native

Scholar-certified cost-plus structures, summarized on one page. No late fees to hide because there are none — lateness triggers help, early settlement earns rebates.

REGULATORY

SAMA-perimeter by design

Examiner-ready governance from the first loan: model cards per version, full decision lineage, fairness testing, no protected proxies.

DATA

PDPL consent from day one

Consent captured honestly at onboarding; k-threshold anonymization built into the platform. Aggregated insights never identify a shop.

RISK

Caps that own survival

Vintage exposure caps, per-market circuit breakers and a Risk function with brake authority — installed before growth pressure exists.
